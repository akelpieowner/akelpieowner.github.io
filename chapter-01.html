Emotional disciplineReciprocityAwareness

In this chapter we will be focusing what I refer to as the tyranny of the irrational.

For the longest time our actions have been impacted by our awareness and outcomes of these actions…

I want us to think of our actions as utility, but not to the common cause, but to our own cause. Furthermore, energy conservation also applies here and it is the best idea to think of it this way: what problems do I want in life? Problems that keep you in motion but never getting anywhere? If yes. Keep reading.

Imagine your problems and options as a circle where arrows from the center go millions of ways. Now the key focus is to reduce the number of arrows and keep the only arrow continuous.

The question you should be asking yourself is... am I repeating leisure activities that keep me feeling good? Or do I randomly stumble upon myself thinking... "Hey I am doing this again and I feel bad about that.."? What I would suggest you do is try to keep your mental projections empty, don't wander off into emotional spirals, keep it simple, keep it focused. Try to imagine nothing and go from there. Projections never help.

What does help is an alignment of your time, energy and frequencies of your actionable interests. Doing something without justification is just self-inflicted martyrdom. So what does one do to escape the trap of that martyrdom? Practice emotional discipline.

Our scarcest resource is time and unfortunately time progresses linearly and some of us think we also do, but imagine if that really were the case. How many times have you seen someone say "I'll do it next week" or "I'll do it next year" or better yet "I'll do it once - insert any given condition". That puts you in a fairly cyclical, linearly forward looking narrative and that robs you of immediate agency. Non-linearity is an essential train of thought to embrace; similarly to your identity. It should never be about a set of fixed attributes, something never changing, but about a constantly, slowly, deliberatley shifting of position yourself in a set of ever-changing circumstances.

As one last core idea here, I want you to consider your relations with the world as reciprocal. To reach and approximate what you want, you have to give in; to know what you want you have to give in; and to experience what you want you also have to give in. The questions is more often than not is that we do not know what we could or are able to give in. Prioritize and likely then just as much you will be able to experience, know and reach.
