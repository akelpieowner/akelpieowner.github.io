"It is evident that over several centuries, social conditions have tended to become more equal, and that in the course of the same period the manners of society have been softened. Are these two things merely contemporaneous, or does any secret link exist between them, so that the one cannot go on without making the other advance?....
In democratic ages men rarely sacrifice themselves for one another, but they display general compassion for the members of the human race. They inflict no useless ills, and they are happy to relieve griefs of others, when they can do so without much hurting themselves. They are not disinterested, but they are humane."
